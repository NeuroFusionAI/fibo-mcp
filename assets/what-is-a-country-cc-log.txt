⏺ Based on the FIBO (Financial Industry Business Ontology) ontology, here's what a country is:

  In FIBO, the concept of country is represented through the SovereignState class:

  Definition: A non-physical juridical entity that is represented by one centralized government that has sovereignty over a
  geographic area.

  Key characteristics:
  - Represented by one centralized government
  - Has a permanent population
  - Has defined territory
  - Has the capacity to enter into relations with other sovereign states

  FIBO also defines related concepts:
  - Federated Sovereignty - a polity characterized by a union of partially self-governing states or regions under a central
  (federal) government
  - Regional Sovereignty - an administrative division or country subdivision that can incur debt, issue contracts, and enter
  into relations with similar entities

  The ontology uses ISO 3166-1 country codes (via https://www.omg.org/spec/LCC/Countries/) to identify specific countries, and
   properties like hasCountry, operatesInCountry, and hasHomeCountry to relate entities to countries.
